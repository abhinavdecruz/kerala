Kerala - God's Own Country

Kerala, often referred to as "God's Own Country," is a state in southern India that captivates visitors with its pristine natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality. This tropical paradise is blessed with lush green landscapes, serene backwaters, golden beaches, and misty hill stations.

The state is renowned for its unique geography - a narrow strip of land sandwiched between the Western Ghats and the Arabian Sea, creating diverse ecosystems from coastal plains to mountainous regions. Kerala's commitment to sustainable tourism and environmental conservation has made it a model for responsible travel.

With a literacy rate of 93.91%, Kerala stands as one of India's most progressive states, combining traditional values with modern development. The state's unique blend of Ayurveda, classical arts, and contemporary lifestyle makes it an unforgettable destination for travelers from around the world.

Rich History & Heritage

3000 BCE

Ancient Kerala

Evidence of early human settlements and trade with ancient civilizations including the Phoenicians, Romans, and Arabs.

1st Century CE

Chera Dynasty

The Chera dynasty established one of the earliest kingdoms in Kerala, with Muziris (present-day Kodungallur) as a major port city.

1498 CE

Vasco da Gama's Arrival

Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama landed in Kappad, marking the beginning of European colonial influence in India.

1956

Formation of Kerala State

Kerala was formed by merging the Malayalam-speaking regions of Travancore, Cochin, and Malabar.

Must-Visit Destinations

Munnar

Famous for its tea plantations, misty mountains, and cool climate. A perfect hill station retreat.

Alleppey Backwaters

Experience the serene backwaters on traditional houseboats, surrounded by coconut palms and paddy fields.

Kovalam Beach

One of India's most famous beaches with golden sand, clear waters, and excellent surfing opportunities.

Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary

Home to elephants, tigers, and diverse wildlife. Take a boat safari on Periyar Lake.

Kochi (Cochin)

A historic port city with Chinese fishing nets, colonial architecture, and vibrant cultural scene.

Varkala Beach

Known for its dramatic cliffs, mineral springs, and spiritual significance as a pilgrimage destination.

Famous Landmarks

Padmanabhaswamy Temple

Located in Thiruvananthapuram, this ancient temple is one of the wealthiest temples in the world, known for its Dravidian architecture and religious significance.

Chinese Fishing Nets

Iconic cantilevered fishing nets in Fort Kochi, introduced by Chinese traders centuries ago, now a symbol of Kerala's maritime heritage.

Mattancherry Palace

Also known as the Dutch Palace, this 16th-century palace in Kochi features beautiful murals depicting scenes from the Ramayana and Mahabharata.

St. Francis Church

One of India's oldest European churches in Fort Kochi, where Vasco da Gama was originally buried, showcasing Portuguese colonial architecture.

Delicious Kerala Cuisine

Kerala's cuisine is a delightful blend of flavors, heavily influenced by its coastal location, tropical climate, and rich cultural heritage. The food is characterized by the generous use of coconut, spices, and rice.

Sadya

A traditional vegetarian feast served on banana leaves during festivals, featuring over 20 dishes including sambar, rasam, and various curries.

Karimeen Pollichathu

Pearl spot fish marinated with spices, wrapped in banana leaves, and grilled to perfection - a signature Kerala delicacy.

Kerala Prawn Curry

Spicy prawn curry cooked in coconut milk with traditional spices, best enjoyed with steamed rice or appam.

Appam & Stew

Soft, lacy rice pancakes served with mild coconut-based vegetable or meat stew, a popular breakfast dish.

Payasam

Traditional rice pudding made with jaggery, coconut milk, and cardamom, often served as dessert during celebrations.

Kerala Tea

Strong, aromatic tea grown in the hills of Munnar and Wayanad, often served with traditional snacks like banana chips.

Rich Flora & Fauna

Kerala's diverse geography supports an incredible variety of flora and fauna. From the Western Ghats' tropical forests to the coastal mangroves, the state is a biodiversity hotspot with numerous endemic species.
